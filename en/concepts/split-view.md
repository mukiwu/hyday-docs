> * Split View lets you open two pieces of content side by side. Whether you're comparing data, referencing across notes, or reading while taking notes, you can do it all in one window. *

## Single View vs. Split View

- **Single view**: The default. Shows one main piece of content at a time.
- **Split View**: Divides the window into left and right panels, each with its own tabs.

## How to Enter Split View

- From the notes overview, click the "…" menu in the top-right corner of a note and choose "Open on the right" or "Open on the left".
- Right-click a backlink on a note or journal entry to open the panel and choose "Open on the right" or "Open on the left".

![Split View: panels displayed side by side](https://hyday.tw/docs/screenshots/concepts-split-view-1.png)

## Typical Use Cases

- **Reference while writing**: Open your reference on the left, write your notes on the right.
- **Compare across dates**: Place two days' journal entries side by side for weekly recaps or periodic reviews.
- **Trace linked context**: Show the current note on one side and a referenced note on the other.

## Independent Tab Management

Both panels support multiple tabs and don't interfere with each other. You can drag tabs around between them to rearrange.

## Adjust the Window Ratio

Drag the divider in the middle to set the ratio between the two panels.

## Back to Single View

Close the last tab in the right panel and the interface automatically goes back to a single column.
