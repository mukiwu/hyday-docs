> *Save frequently used filter conditions as**pinned filters**and switch between them straight from the sidebar.*

## 1. Build a Filter Condition

Click**Filter**at the top of the sidebar to open the panel. Combine as many conditions as you need, for example:

- **Tag**: includes`#idea`
- **Note type**:`article`or`card`

The list filters live as you adjust the settings.

## 2. Pin the Filter

At the bottom of the panel, click**Pin this filter**, give it a name (such as**This week's inspiration**) and an icon, then click Save.

![How pinned filters appear in the sidebar](https://hyday.tw/docs/screenshots/guides-pin-filters-1.png)

## 3. Switch Filters with One Click

Pinned filters live in the sidebar permanently. Click the name to instantly switch the list to that filter's results.

## Edit and Delete

Hover a pinned filter in the sidebar and click the "..." on the right to edit, delete, or reorder it.

> **ℹ️ Info**
> Pinned filters replace traditional hierarchical folder thinking. The same note can show up under many filters — that's the key to Hyday's flexible knowledge management.
