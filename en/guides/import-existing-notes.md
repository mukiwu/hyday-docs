> * Whether you're migrating from Obsidian, Bear, Notion, or Apple Notes, the core idea is the same: export your data as Markdown, then open the folder in Hyday. *

## Migrating from Obsidian

Hyday ships with a dedicated migration flow for Obsidian users:

1. On Hyday's launch screen, click **Add folder** → Import from Obsidian
2. Follow the wizard to bring in your notes, journal entries, and images. Hyday automatically recognizes `.md` files, frontmatter metadata, and `[[]]` backlinks.

> **⚠️ Warning**
>  Hyday doesn't yet support Obsidian's block-level backlinks (e.g. `[[note#^block-id]]`). These appear as broken links. 

If you didn't import Obsidian files during the initial setup, you can still go to Settings → Import and pick an Obsidian folder later.

![Import from Obsidian](https://hyday.tw/docs/screenshots/guides-import-existing-notes-1.png)

## Migrating from Other Note Apps

Apart from Obsidian, Hyday doesn't build dedicated converters. Use a third-party tool to convert your notes to Markdown first, then drop them into Hyday's notes folder.

> **ℹ️ Info**
>  Once you've moved in, try keeping a daily journal in Hyday for a week, experience the LifeLog marks, and build a few backlinks across notes. Only by using it for real will you feel how Hyday differs from a traditional note app in the dimension of thinking.
